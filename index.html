Book Summary


We open with the protagonist, Inman hospitalized from grave war wound. From his experiences fighting in the war. Inman yearns to return to Ada, his lover.

Meanwhile, Ada is back at Black Cove farm by herself doesn’t know what to do. Ada’s neighbor sends over a girl named Ruby to help out around the farm, as Ada is well equipped to manage it. They quickly become friends.

Inman’s journey isn’t as pleasant. After being pursued across a river along with other hardships, Solomon Veassy, an exiled preacher joins Inman. Inman and Veassy carry out a variety of tasks as they travel.

At the town Cold Mountain, Ada reveals her intricate past including her the relationship and deaths of her parents. Stobrod, Ruby’s father, tries to convince Ruby that he had changed his ways, but Ruby isn’t convinced.

Going back to Inman’s storyline, Inman continues to be subjected to obstacles. Through the kindness of a slave and an old woman, Inman continues on until he is again sheltered by a kind young woman.

Inman finally reaches Cold Mountain. He see Ada’s tracks and follows them. Ada doesn’t recognize Inman at first, but they then reunite. The Guardsmen attacked Stobrod earlier appear and attack them. Stobrod successfully escape while Inman kills three of the men. The boy shoots Inman, and by the time the others returned, Inman was dead.

Although Ada has lost Inman, she has a nine-year old child and spends her days attending to her child and her farm.
